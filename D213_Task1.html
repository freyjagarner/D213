Part II: Method Justification¶

B. Summarize the assumptions of a time series model including stationarity and autocorrelated data.¶

The assumptions of the time series model as are follows:

  • The patterns found in the analysis are expected to match future results.

  • The data are stationary, meaning that the mean, variance and autocorrelation structure do not change over time. (Nist Sematech Stationarity)

  • There are no outliers

  • There is no correlated random error.

E. Summarize your findings and assumptions, including the following points:¶

1. Discuss the results of your data analysis, including the following:¶

  • the selection of an ARIMA model

We used auto_rima for find the optimal p or order of AR, q or order of MA, and d or differencing required to make the data stationary values of 1, 0 and 0 respectively. The ARIMA model is good for data that has a clear pattern, and no seasonality which makes it a good fit for the data that we have.

  • the prediction interval of the forecast

The prediction interval is one day.

  • a justification of the forecast length

This model bases it's predictions off of recent past predictions and expects that trend to be followed, and so making longer predictions may be inaccurate.

  • the model evaluation procedure and error metric

The mean squared error for this model is 9.398028, The RMSE is 3.065620
